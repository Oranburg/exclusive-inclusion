Learning Resources

Syllabi, lecture notes, teaching guides, and supplementary readings for educators and students incorporating this research into courses on administrative law, organizational theory, employment law, or critical legal studies.

Learning Objectives

By engaging with this paper and accompanying materials, students and practitioners should be able to:

  1. Identify the structural mechanisms by which inclusion-oriented institutional commitments can produce identity-based exclusionary outcomes.
  2. Apply organizational theory and administrative law frameworks to analyze DEI programs.
  3. Evaluate proposed reforms to DEI structures using the paper’s analytical framework.
  4. Distinguish between first-order inclusion commitments and the second-order organizational dynamics they produce.
  5. Construct policy arguments for institutional reform based on the paper’s findings.
