Exclusive Inclusion

Activities & Exercises

Activities

Discussion prompts, hypotheticals, classroom exercises, and structured debates that bring the paper’s core arguments to life. These activities are designed for law school seminars, faculty workshops, and individual study.

Discussion Prompt

The Paradox in Practice

Consider an institution that has adopted a comprehensive DEI program. Identify three ways the program might, despite its stated goals, produce identity-based exclusion. What structural factors contribute to this outcome?

Available
Hypothetical

The Faculty Hiring Committee

A law school’s faculty hiring committee is charged with increasing diversity. Analyze how its operating procedures might produce exclusionary outcomes even when all members are sincerely committed to inclusion.

Structured Debate

DEI Programs: Reform or Abolish?

A structured debate in which teams argue for comprehensive reform of existing DEI programs versus abolition and replacement with alternative approaches. Includes role assignments, evidence packets, and debrief questions.

Case Study

Organizational Case Analysis

Apply the paper’s analytical framework to a real-world organizational case. Identify the mechanisms of exclusion, the institutional incentives that sustain them, and possible reform pathways.

Reflective Exercise

Mapping Your Institution

A structured self-assessment exercise for administrators and faculty: identify inclusion commitments at your institution and trace the formal and informal mechanisms by which they may produce exclusionary outcomes.

Policy Workshop

Drafting Inclusive Bylaws

Using the paper’s framework, draft bylaw provisions for a fictional institution designed to avoid the structural causes of inclusive exclusion. Compare drafts and identify persistent vulnerabilities.
