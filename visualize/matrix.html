Q3: Contested Ground
Individual / Identity
Individual action targeting a protected identity group. May trigger Title VII, Title IX, or campus conduct codes depending on severity and pattern. Governance void is less directly implicated.
Q4: Academic Autonomy
Institutional / Perspective
Institutional action based on viewpoint or perspective. Academic freedom doctrine and Sweezy / Keyishian protections apply. Not the primary site of exclusive inclusion harm.

The Jurisdictional Retreat3

The Retreat occurs when an actor reframes institutional, identity-targeted conduct (Q2) as individual, perspective-based expression (Q1) to escape civil rights accountability.

Actual Conduct
Q2: Institutional / Identity
Departmental authority deployed via official budget, hiring, speaker-selection, or curriculum to systematically exclude members of a protected group.
→
Actor's Reframing
Q1: Individual / Perspective
Actor claims personal academic freedom, individual expression, or viewpoint-based discretion — retreating from institutional accountability to individual immunity.

"The FAIR doctrine forecloses this retreat: institutional deployment of official authority is conduct regulation, not speech, regardless of its expressive dimensions."4
